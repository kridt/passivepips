🔍 **What Is Leverage in Forex and How Does It Affect PAMM?** 📈

In the world of forex trading, leverage is a powerful tool that allows traders to control larger positions than their capital would otherwise permit. But what exactly does this mean, and how does it influence PAMM (Percentage Allocation Management Module) trading?

**Forex leverage explained:** Think of leverage as a multiplier. For instance, if you have a leverage ratio of 100:1, you can control $100,000 with just $1,000 of your own capital. This can significantly amplify your potential profits, but it also comes with increased risk. 

In a PAMM setup, where funds from multiple investors are pooled and managed by a skilled trader, leverage can enhance both gains and losses. While a well-executed trade can yield impressive returns, even minor market fluctuations can lead to substantial losses if leveraged positions move against you.

It's crucial to understand the **leverage risk** involved and to work with experienced PAMM managers who apply sound risk management strategies.
